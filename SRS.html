SRS-2 Information

1.1 Purpose

Identity theft and phishing are the common dangers of online shopping. Secure Socket Layer (SSL) encryption prevents the interception of consumer information in transit between the consumer and the online merchant. However, one must still trust merchant and its employees not to use consumer information for their own purchases and not to sell the information to others.


We hereby propose a new method, that uses steganography (text based) and visual cryptography, which minimizes information sharing between consumer and online merchant but enable successful fund transfer from consumer’s account to merchant’s account thereby safeguarding consumer information and preventing misuse of information at merchant side.

SRS-4 Information

1.3 Product perspective

A rapid growth in E-Commerce market is seen in recent time throughout the world. With ever increasing popularity of online shopping, Debit or Credit card fraud and personal information security are major concerns for customers, merchants and banks specifically in the case of CNP (Card Not Present). This paper presents a new approach for providing limited information only that is necessary for fund transfer during online shopping thereby safeguarding customer data and increasing customer confidence and preventing identity theft. The method uses combined application of steganography and visual cryptography for this purpose.

    2. Decryption --> stacking all the shares to retrieve secret image

2.2 Functions

Encryption

  • Description and Priority
  1. This Feature is used by both steganography and visual cryptography.
  2. Secret key is first to be converted into unique code using Vedic codes and visual cryptography converts image into shares.


  • Stimulus/Response Sequences :
  1. Registration of user which generates shares
  2. Right choice of shares to be done while making payment


Decryption

  • Description and Priority
  1. This feature involves decryption algorithm using visual cryptography.
  2. Once all the shares are combined to make one single image and then it is decrypted we get a normal image
  3. Which is then is to be decoded to get secret key.
  4. This secret key is then sent to bank.


  • Stimulus/Response Sequences:
  1. Search for particular share in database.
  2. Combining it with consumers’ Shares.
  3. Sending the key to Bank Verification

2.6 Design constraints

  1. During payment, merchant’s payment system requires to direct the shopper to CA’s portal but fraudulent merchant may direct shopper to a portal similar to CA’s portal but of its own making and get hold of customer own share.
  2. Need to carry the secret image share to authenticate the payment process
  3. Use of text steganography involves encoding using Vedic Codes which is indeed described for alphabets. In this case, the key which will be used by the consumer to generate shares must contain alphabets.
